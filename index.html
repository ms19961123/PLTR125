Company Background

Building the World's Most
Important Data Company

From a post-9/11 intelligence mission to a publicly-traded AI powerhouse, Palantir has redefined what software platforms can achieve.

Founded in 2003 by Peter Thiel, Alex Karp, Joe Lonsdale, Stephen Cohen, and Nathan Gettings, Palantir Technologies was born from a conviction that technology could protect civil liberties while empowering institutions to make better decisions. Named after the all-seeing stones in Tolkien's Lord of the Rings, the company set out to build software that could integrate and analyze vast, disparate data sets to uncover hidden patterns and actionable intelligence.

For its first decade, Palantir operated largely in the shadows, serving intelligence agencies and the U.S. military with its Gotham platform. The software played critical roles in counter-terrorism operations, fraud detection, and disaster response. This deep government relationship created an unassailable moat — battle-tested technology trusted at the highest classification levels.

Under Dr. Alexander Karp's unconventional leadership — he holds a Ph.D. in philosophy from Goethe University Frankfurt — Palantir pursued a long-term vision rather than chasing quarterly metrics. This patient approach paid off: the company went public via direct listing on September 30, 2020, and has since accelerated its commercial expansion with the Foundry and AIP (Artificial Intelligence Platform) products.

Today, Palantir operates three core platforms: Gotham for government and defense, Foundry for commercial enterprises, and AIP which brings large language models directly into operational workflows. The AIP platform has been a game-changer, driving explosive demand as organizations race to deploy AI in production environments.

A data-driven case for Palantir's realistic valuation

ARGUMENT 01

Accelerating Revenue Growth with Expanding TAM

Palantir's Total Addressable Market has expanded dramatically with AIP. The company's FY2024 revenue hit $2.87B with 26% YoY growth, accelerating from 17% in 2023. Commercial revenue alone grew 36% as enterprise adoption accelerates. At a sustained 25%+ growth trajectory, revenue reaching $5B+ by 2027 supports a significantly higher valuation floor.

📈 26% YoY Revenue Growth Accelerating
ARGUMENT 02

Operating Leverage & Margin Expansion

Palantir achieved GAAP operating margins of ~16% in 2024, up from near-zero in 2022. The software-centric model with high retention rates (NRR 115%+) creates natural operating leverage. As revenue scales, margins should expand toward 25-30% — each new customer adds revenue with minimal marginal cost, driving substantial free cash flow expansion.

💰 ~16% GAAP Operating Margin
ARGUMENT 03

AI Platform Moat & First-Mover Advantage

While competitors build AI tools, Palantir builds the ontology layer that makes AI actually work in production. AIP doesn't just offer a chatbot — it integrates LLMs into complex operational workflows with guardrails, access controls, and real-time data. This infrastructure moat is nearly impossible to replicate, creating sticky, mission-critical deployments with multi-year contracts.

🤖 500+ AIP Boot Camps Completed
ARGUMENT 04

Government Tailwinds & Defense Spending

U.S. defense spending continues to rise with increased focus on AI/ML integration. Palantir holds strategic contracts including the Army's TITAN program ($178M) and multiple CDAO initiatives. Allied nations are increasingly adopting Palantir platforms. Government revenue provides a stable, recurring base that de-risks the overall business model — currently ~55% of total revenue.

🌎 $1.58B Government Revenue FY2024
